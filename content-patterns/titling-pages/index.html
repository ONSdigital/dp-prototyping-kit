Titling your pages

Page title

Page titles need to be short, clear, and reflect the content on the page. Both appear in search results and in the table of contents on each page in a release.

Think about how useful your title is in each of those contexts

For example

Employment

Latest estimates of the number of people paid to work in the UK.

If the page summarises a number of different bulletins in a release, it's an "overview".

For example

Labour market overview

Latest estimates of employment, unemployment, economic inactivity and other employment-related statistics for the UK

Page subtitles

Tell users what to expect to find on this page. Describe the topic the page covers, but remember this is not the definition (we have a glossary for that).

You should include the geographic coverage, if appropriate.

For example

Crime overview

Crimes against households, adults, children, businesses and society in England and Wales.
